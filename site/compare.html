The verdict

Who should use what

No tool is right for everyone. Here's our honest assessment of which freelancer profile each tool serves best.

Best for
⚡ Clientli
Freelancers who want one connected system instead of seven separate tools — and who value time tracking, proposals, and analytics as much as invoicing.
  • Everything connected in one system
  • Best-in-class time → invoice workflow
  • Native desktop app (Linux, Mac, Windows)
  • Free plan with no card required
  • Never takes a % of your invoices
  • Younger product — some edge cases still maturing
  • Mobile app still in development
Best for
📒 FreshBooks
Freelancers who primarily need strong accounting features and already use a separate time tracker and project tool.
  • Strong accounting & bookkeeping features
  • Good reporting for tax prep
  • Established, mature platform
  • No proposals or contract management
  • Time tracking is basic
  • No free plan — starts at $19/mo
  • No native desktop app
Best for
🌾 Harvest
Freelancers whose primary need is precise time tracking and who are happy to use separate tools for everything else.
  • Best pure time tracking in the market
  • Strong project budget tracking
  • Relatively affordable
  • No proposals, contracts, or client CRM
  • No free plan
  • Invoicing is secondary, not primary
Context for this comparison

How was this comparison built?

We tested every tool with a test freelance account over 30 days in January 2025. Where features are listed as partial or basic, we've tested them specifically and found meaningful limitations relative to what the category requires. We've tried to be as fair to competitors as we are to ourselves.

Is this comparison biased?

We're the Clientli team — so yes, we have a perspective. We've tried to be accurate about where competitors are stronger (FreshBooks' accounting depth, Harvest's time tracking maturity) and where Clientli is still catching up (mobile app, some accounting edge cases). We welcome corrections.

What about HoneyBook?

HoneyBook is strongest for service-based freelancers who prioritise client experience and workflow automation — especially photographers, event planners, and creative agencies. It's less suited for freelancers who need granular time tracking or deep analytics. It's also the most expensive option at $39–$79/month.

What about Wave's free plan?

Wave is genuinely free for invoicing and accounting, which makes it attractive for freelancers just starting out. The main limitations are the 1% payment processing surcharge, no time tracking, no proposals, and no client CRM. It's a good starting point — but most growing freelancers outgrow it within a year.
